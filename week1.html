Here are some examples of user instructions that I found (some that I actually use(d) myself!):

OCAD saddle stitch & print instructions:

    This is a fairly detailed set of instructions for setting up an InDesign doc for print (specifically for turning into a saddle stitch booklet).
    I love the little numbered pages it helps visualize the layout of the document, and has saved me multiple times lol.
    Straightforward, clean, simple, addresses what settings you need and has pretty throrough notes to prevent you from messing up.

Google Home / Google Nest setup instructions:

    A boring but to-the-point setup guide for configuring the Google Nest & Home (i have the home from when spotify gave it away for free :3).
    It assumes that the user has an Android for some reason (?) not sure why.
    The one thing I will commend is their use of the actual UI buttons you'd see in the app being in the instructions for clarity.

Ikea shelf disassembly guide (very obvious choice but atleast I chose disassembly over assembly):

    It blends whimsy with practicality as you can clearly tell what's happening, what you need, and how to do it (all while being fairly minimal).
    Great and fun use of visuals
